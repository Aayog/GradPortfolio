Research

Thesis Project

Title: View Interpolation for 360° Panoramic Spherical Images Using Stitched Multiplane Image Corridors

Description: In this research project, we focus on developing a robust view interpolation technique for 360° panoramic spherical images by utilizing stitched multiplane image corridors. The primary objective is to enhance the visual experience of virtual reality and immersive environments by generating smoother transitions between different viewpoints within the 360° scene. To achieve this, we first create a corridor of multiplane images, which are derived from the original panoramic images by dividing them into multiple layers based on depth information. This depth-based segmentation enables us to represent the 3D structure of the scene more accurately, allowing for better handling of occlusions and parallax effects. Next, we stitch these multiplane images together to form a continuous corridor that encompasses the entire 360° scene. This step involves addressing challenges such as correcting misalignments, blending image seams, and maintaining spatial consistency throughout the corridor. Finally, we apply advanced machine learning algorithms to interpolate novel views within the stitched multiplane image corridor. These algorithms take into account both spatial and angular information, ensuring a seamless transition between viewpoints while preserving the original scene's fidelity. By employing this view interpolation method, we aim to provide an enhanced visual experience in virtual reality and immersive environments, where users can smoothly navigate through the 360° scene with minimal distortion or artifacts. This research has the potential to greatly impact applications in industries such as gaming, tourism, and remote collaboration, where realistic and seamless immersion is crucial for user engagement and satisfaction.
